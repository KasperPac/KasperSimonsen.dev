02 Pattern reference

Pattern reference

Copy what you need from below. Delete this whole section before final render.

A standalone H2 heading

Body paragraph after a plain H2.

Section · 01

H2 with mono eyebrow above

Use the .h2-block wrapper when you want a small mono uppercase tag introducing the heading. Especially good for ordered phases ("Stage 01 — Months 0–6").

H3 — orange mono uppercase section label

H3s are smaller and feel more like labels than headings. Use sparingly — they break up a long section without being heavy.

Bullet list — em-dash bullets

  • The brand bullet is an em-dash in mono dim grey.
  • Don't use plain HTML bullets — they won't pick up the brand styling.
  • Always use <ul class="brand">.

Numbered list — leading-zero counters

  1. Numbered lists have mono leading-zero counters (01, 02, 03).
  2. Use for sequential steps, ranked items, or open questions where order matters.
  3. Don't mix numbered and bulleted lists in the same logical group.

Callout label · mono uppercase

This is the standard callout block. Orange left bar, dark surface fill, mono uppercase label in orange. Use for design insights, key risks, or single-sentence "this is the important bit" interjections.

Multi-paragraph callouts are fine — paragraphs separate naturally.
